<p><p>Thermoelectric module assemblies utilize different cooling methods to maintain optimal performance. Air-cooling systems use ambient air to dissipate heat, offering simple installation and maintenance but limited efficiency in high-heat applications. Liquid-cooling employs circulating fluids to enhance heat transfer, providing superior performance and efficiency, especially in compact designs. Direct contact cooling uses materials that provide direct thermal conduction, ensuring minimal thermal resistance and maximizing heat dissipation. Each cooling type is chosen based on specific application needs and environmental conditions.</p></p>
