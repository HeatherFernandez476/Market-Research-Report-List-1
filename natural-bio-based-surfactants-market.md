<p><p>The natural bio-based surfactants market is categorized into four types: Anionic, Nonionic, Cationic, and Amphoteric natural surfactants. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge, making them effective cleansers and emulsifiers. Nonionic surfactants are neutral, offering mildness and versatility in formulations. Cationic surfactants possess a positive charge, often used for conditioning and antibacterial properties. Amphoteric surfactants can act as either cationic or anionic depending on the pH, providing flexibility in various applications, particularly in personal care and cleaning products.</p></p>
