<p><strong>The Remote Airborne Particle Counters Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Ionising</li><li>Non-ionising</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Remote airborne particle counters are categorized into ionizing and non-ionizing types based on their detection methods. Ionizing counters use radiation to detect particle charges, making them highly sensitive and suitable for monitoring low concentrations of particles. In contrast, non-ionizing counters operate through light scattering techniques, relying on laser or LED light to capture particle sizes and concentrations. Both types serve critical roles in industries like pharmaceuticals, aerospace, and cleanroom environments by ensuring air quality and regulatory compliance.</p></p>
