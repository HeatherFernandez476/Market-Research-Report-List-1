<p><p>The H. pylori noninvasive diagnostic tests market includes three main types: Serology tests, Stool antigen tests, and Urea breath tests. Serology tests detect antibodies to H. pylori in the blood, indicating infection. Stool antigen tests identify H. pylori antigens in fecal samples, providing a reliable diagnosis. Urea breath tests measure carbon dioxide in exhaled breath after ingestion of a urea solution, confirming active infection. These noninvasive methods are preferred for their ease of use, accuracy, and patient comfort.</p></p>
